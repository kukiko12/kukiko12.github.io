About Me

I am a Master's student in Particle and Nuclear Physics at Fudan University, working within the STCF collaboration. My research focuses on charm and flavor physics, with particular interest in semileptonic decays and Cabibbo-suppressed processes. I have experience in data analysis using ROOT and C++, including statistical analysis, Monte Carlo simulation, and systematic uncertainty evaluation. I am seeking PhD opportunities in experimental particle physics, especially in flavor physics and collider-based experiments.

Research Interests

Charm physics, semileptonic decays, detector optimization, statistical analysis, utilized machine learning methods to improve background discrimination and noise suppression.
